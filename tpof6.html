📖 Ten Percent of Forever · Chapter 6

The Fight

He found her sketchbook. He found everything.

Romance Slice of Life By AKA

"He stood alone — shoes soaked from pacing."

Three days. She didn't come.

It was raining again.

The vending machine roof dripped steadily, but this time Hiro stood alone — hands in his pockets, hood pulled up, shoes soaked from pacing.

He hadn't seen Mei in three days.
Not really.
Not in the way that mattered.

She was there, technically — in class, at her desk, in the same building — but her eyes never met his. Her laughter never returned. And the space between them grew like fog.

He had apologized. Twice.
But words didn't erase silence. Not when silence was a wall she chose to hide behind.

So Hiro waited.
And for the first time since that first umbrella morning, she didn't come.

The bell rang. He turned to leave.
Then — he saw it.

A sketchbook. Left on the bench. Slightly wet at the corners.

His heart clenched.
She would never leave it behind.

He picked it up, gently brushing the raindrops off the cover. It was worn, soft at the edges, the pages thick with pencil marks and smudges. Part of him hesitated — it wasn't his to open.

But then again… maybe it was meant for him.

So he turned the pages.

The first few were things he expected — landscapes, classroom corners, rooftops under starry skies.

Then he saw them.

Drawings of him.
Dozens.

✏️ Pages of him
  • ●Leaning against the vending machine with his hood up.
  • ●Laughing during lunch.
  • ●Their shoes side by side beneath the firework-lit sky.
📄 One page, titled Ten Percent of Forever

Two people walking in the rain, under a single umbrella.
No faces. Just closeness. Just silence that meant something.

Hiro closed the sketchbook gently, hands shaking.

And then he ran.

He found her on the third floor, just about to enter the art room. Hoodie damp from the weather, sketchbook not in her arms.

"Mei!"

She turned, startled.
He walked up, holding it out.
"You forgot this."

Her eyes widened. She reached for it slowly, then paused.
"…You opened it?"
He nodded. "I did."

She stayed quiet, waiting.

Hiro took a breath.
"…I meant what I said. Before. About giving me ten percent of your forever."

Her fingers curled tighter around the sketchbook.

"I was stupid. And scared. But not because I didn't feel something. I do. I do, Mei. And if you'll let me—"

He didn't finish.

Because she stepped forward.
And hugged him.

🤍 Right there in the hallway

Quiet. Soft. Real.
Like the kind of hug that didn't need words.
Just presence.

"…Okay," she whispered.
