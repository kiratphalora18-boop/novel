A Name Beneath the Ink

Thursday

Thursday came with unexpected warmth.
The sakura tree had begun to shift — just a little. Enough to soften the landscape. Enough to make Nao notice the pink bleeding slowly back into the world.

She was early that day.
Maybe too early. She hadn't even gone to class yet. Her bag still slung on one shoulder, coffee in hand, she knelt down at the bench like it was instinct now.

There it was: the book, and another note.
But this one looked different.
Neater. Folded with more care than before. And on the front, a single word:

"Nao."

Her full name. Not just the initial.
Her breath caught.
She opened it slowly.

🌸   ·   🌸
Letter from Haruki
Nao,

I didn't think I'd write your name like this.
But it felt wrong not to.

Yesterday, I saw you.
Not clearly. Just… a glimpse.
But something about the way you looked up at the tree — like you were listening for something — made me wonder if I'd met you before.

I probably haven't.
But it feels like I have.

If you're still okay with it — yes.
I'd like to know you.

— Haruki
· · ·

Haruki.
She said it aloud under her breath.
There was something about the name that made sense.
She didn't know him.

And yet…
The way he wrote — the quiet rhythm of it — felt like sitting beside someone on a rainy afternoon. Not speaking, but not needing to.
She stared at the letter for a while, her heart skipping in uneven beats.
Then, slowly, she smiled.

She pulled out her notebook. No tearing this time — she wrote carefully, slowly, as if each word mattered.

Letter from Nao
Haruki,

I think we met too late.
But maybe that's still better than never.

Let's start with the small things.
What's your favorite book?

(And can we keep pretending we're still strangers writing through time, just for a little longer?)

— Nao
🌸   ·   🌸

She folded it. Tucked it into the same page of the paperback. Closed the floorboard gently, like sealing something fragile.
When she turned to leave, she glanced once more at the sakura branches above.
And this time, there were petals.
Not many.
Just enough to know that spring was real.

A Place the Wind
Can't Reach

Friday · Rain

Haruki.
Nao found herself mouthing the name as she walked to class, like it was a secret she wasn't ready to say aloud.
She passed him in the hall once. She didn't know if he recognized her. He didn't look her way. Or maybe he did. Just quickly. Just enough.

But each time she left a note, it was there. His reply. His rhythm. His way of saying things that made the world feel softer.
They never acknowledged it outside the pages.
That was the rule.
And somehow, it made everything more real.

That Friday, it rained.
Not hard. Just the kind of rain that turned your thoughts quiet.
She went to the bench anyway. Umbrella half-closed, scarf loose around her neck.
The book was dry. The letter inside was warm.

· · ·
Letter from Haruki
Nao,

I thought about your question. My favorite book.

It changes, but lately?
The Little Prince.
I like that it's about something simple — a boy and a rose — and yet says everything about growing up, loneliness, and how we leave people behind even when we love them.

What about you?

And…
If we ever did meet — really meet — would we still write letters after?

— Haruki
🌸   ·   🌸

She read the last question twice. Then once more.
Would we still write letters?
She didn't know.

Part of her wanted to keep hiding behind paper forever. It was safe here. Easier to be honest.
But a part of her — the one that trembled every time she thought of his name — wanted more.
Wanted real.

She didn't answer right away.
Instead, she sat on the bench for a long time, umbrella closed at her side, letting the rain tap against her sleeves.
Somewhere behind her, someone passed by. Quiet steps. Not rushing.
She didn't look up.
But her heart skipped anyway.

Some questions don't need answering right away.
Some silences say more than letters ever could.
