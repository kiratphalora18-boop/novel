🌸 The Book with No Cover · Chapter 1

The Book
with No Cover

She wasn't there for books. Just quiet.

Romance Slice of Life By AKA
February · Still cold
🌸 The sakura tree

The library was closed. But Nao didn't care.

The library was closed for renovation. But Nao didn't care.

She wasn't there for books today. Just quiet.

The rain had just stopped, and the sky wore that washed-out, tired look it always did in February. Cold clung to her sleeves as she sat beneath the sakura tree, tugging her coat tighter.

This was her third week at the new school.
No one noticed her. She preferred it that way.

Then, as she shifted on the old bench, her heel tapped something underneath. Hollow.

Curious, she reached down — fingers brushing against a loose wooden plank — and pulled.

📖
A hidden gap beneath the bench. Small. Worn. A paperback with no cover.

The pages were yellowed, soft at the edges like it had been read a hundred times. She flipped through it. At the center, between chapters, was a folded piece of paper.

Her name wasn't on it.

But she read it anyway.

📜 The letter inside

Hey, Sora. You were right. I shouldn't have waited. But I still did. Not because I hoped you'd come back. But because I wanted you to know — Someone stayed. Even when you couldn't. And that mattered. It still does.

Nao stared at the words.

Her throat tightened, suddenly and without warning.

She didn't know who Sora was. Or the person who wrote the letter.

But the ache behind it — the quiet heartbreak — felt familiar.
Like something she'd tried to forget.
Like something she hadn't told anyone.

The book trembled slightly in her hands.

Then, without really thinking, she reached into her bag, tore a page from her notebook, and began to write.

✏️ Nao's reply

I don't know who you are. But thank you for this. For staying, even if it wasn't for me.

She folded it. Slipped it between the pages. And tucked the book gently back beneath the floorboard.

It felt like something sacred. Like a secret only she was allowed to carry.

As she stood up and dusted her skirt, Nao glanced back at the bench.

The wind stirred slightly, lifting a few sakura petals from the ground — though it wasn't spring.

And for the first time since arriving at this new school, she didn't feel entirely alone.

🌸

Someone Replied

She told herself she wasn't going back.

The next day, she told herself she wasn't going back.

She wasn't the type to chase ghosts or believe in fate. Whatever that letter was — a forgotten goodbye, a message lost to time — it wasn't hers to touch again.

But by lunch, her feet were already leading her there.

Back to the sakura tree.
Back to the bench.
Back to the book with no cover.

She knelt, opened the floorboard.
The paperback was still there.

But something had changed.

Another note was tucked beside her own. Folded neatly, with handwriting that wasn't hers.

Nao's heart skipped.

She looked around.
No one.

She picked up the new paper and unfolded it slowly.

📜 Someone wrote back

You read it. I thought maybe no one ever would. Or maybe it'd crumble with time. I don't know who you are. But thanks for writing back. It's been a long time since someone left anything here.

Nao sat down without realizing it. Her hands trembled slightly — not from fear, but from something deeper. A connection forming where there wasn't supposed to be one.

She pulled out her pen.

✏️ Nao writes back

I didn't mean to intrude. But it felt like the letter was waiting for someone. Maybe both of us. Did you know them? Sora? The one the letter was for?

– N

Sliding the new note between the pages, she placed the book back and closed the floorboard again.

She stood, brushing her skirt off, but didn't walk away immediately.

Instead, she looked up at the sakura tree, bare as ever in the lingering cold.

And wondered what kind of heartbreak could be buried under a tree and still bloom years later.
