🌂 The Other Side of Rain

The Girl Who
Held It Slightly
to His Side

He noticed everything she tried to hide.

Romance Slice of Life By AKA

The umbrella-sharing became routine.

Not every day. Not always under gray skies. But often enough that people started whispering.

💬 What people said
—"Minato? With her?"
—"She talks to him?"

But Rika didn't care.
Or maybe she pretended not to.

She still laughed loudly with her friends during class, still got scolded for not doing her homework, still played the role everyone expected of her.

Except when it rained.

Then… she changed.

That's what Minato noticed first. How quiet she became when the streets turned wet. How she always held the umbrella just slightly more to his side. How she smiled… but softer. Sadder.

· · ·

One afternoon, as thunder grumbled in the distance and the sky turned a sick shade of violet, Minato walked toward their usual meeting spot — the vending machine behind the gym.

She wasn't there.

At first, he thought she left early. Then maybe sick. But something didn't sit right.

So he searched.

He checked the corridors. The rooftop. Even peeked into the music room.
Empty.

Then, near the back stairwell — the one no one used — he heard it.

A voice. Choked. Cracked. Not loud, but shaking.

🌩 The back stairwell · No one used it

A voice. Choked. Not loud — shaking.

He turned the corner.

There she was — Rika, sitting on the floor, hugging her knees, mascara running like ink.

🌂
Her umbrella — snapped beside her. Like it had been thrown. Her mom's. Not hers.

She didn't see him at first.

Not until he knelt beside her and whispered,
"Rika…"

She flinched. Then looked up, eyes red, and whispered the words no one expected from a girl like her:

"I'm so tired of pretending I'm okay."

Minato didn't speak. He just sat beside her, legs stretched out, rain softly tapping on the old stairwell window above.

Rika wiped her face with the sleeve of her uniform, like it didn't matter. Like she didn't matter.

"Sorry," she muttered. "You weren't supposed to see that."
"You're crying," Minato said gently. "You don't have to apologize."

She gave a hollow laugh.

🎭 What she said about herself
  • ●"People like me don't get to fall apart."
  • ●We're the fun ones.
  • ●The glue.
  • ●The noise.
  • ●The comic relief.
  • ●Not tonight.

Rika stared down at her umbrella — one side bent, the handle cracked.
"I broke it," she said, voice small. "It wasn't even mine. It was my mom's. She told me to take care of it."

He finally spoke.

🗣 Minato

"…Then maybe it wasn't strong enough to protect you."

He wasn't smiling. He wasn't joking.

For some reason, those words hit harder than anything anyone had ever said to her.

"I'm not good at talking," Minato continued, "but I'm good at noticing."

He pulled something from his bag.

🧻 What he gave her

A napkin. Crumpled, but clean.

She stared at it for a second, then took it and wiped her eyes again — slower this time.

"…Why do you walk with me?" she asked. "I don't make it easy."

He glanced at her. Rain cast reflections across her face like fractured glass.

"Because you always forget your umbrella," he said.

A beat. Then, softer:

"And because no one else sees what I see."

She looked down, blinking fast.

· · ·

They didn't say much else. Just sat there. Two soaked high schoolers with too much to carry and not enough words to carry it with.

✦ But sometimes

Being seen is enough.
