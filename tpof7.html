Ten Percent of Forever
Chapter Ten

The Last Morning

7:10 AM
10%

The school year was ending.
Desks were already being rearranged. Uniforms had started to feel too tight or too loose. Yearbooks were being passed around with halfhearted jokes scribbled into margins.
Everything felt like it was moving too fast.
Except for this moment.

7:10 AM.
The vending machine roof still leaked slightly in the corner.
The sky was a soft grey — not raining, not sunny, just… somewhere in between.
Hiro stood there, hands in his pockets, breathing slow.
And then — footsteps.

She came into view, hair slightly windblown, blazer wrinkled, sketchbook tucked carefully beneath her arm.
Mei.
"Morning," she said, stepping under the roof beside him.
"You're late," he teased.
"You're early."
He smiled. "You came."
"Of course I did," she said.

— 10% —

Then held up a folded piece of paper.
"This is yours."
He blinked. "What's this?"
"I drew the first day. The umbrella day."
She pressed it into his hands before he could react.
Then added, "It felt right to give it back. You started it."

Mei's drawing — the first day
Two figures under an umbrella, but this time… their faces were visible.
Smiling.

Mei looked away, suddenly shy.
"You kept showing up," she said softly. "Even after I pushed you away. Even when I was scared."
"I was scared too."
"But you were kind," she whispered. "Not loud. Not dramatic. Just… here. Every time."

— 10% —

A pause.
Then Hiro took a deep breath.
Reached into his bag.
And pulled out an umbrella.
"Want to walk with me?" he asked.
She stared for a second — like she might cry, or laugh, or both.
Then nodded.
And reached out her hand to hold the handle with him.

They stepped into the morning together.
No rush. No noise.
Just two people walking side by side.
No promises of forever.
No need for them.
Just this moment.
This quiet, gentle, ten percent of forever.
And it was enough.

10%
"Ten percent of forever is still forever,
if you feel it right."
The End
