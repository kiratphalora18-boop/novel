🌧 The Day She Stayed · Chapter 5

The Day the Sky
Stopped Moving

Room 307. The call. The fight to stay.

Romance Drama Sad By AKA

It happened on a Wednesday.

Ren had just finished developing the photo she secretly took of him — the one with the soft light and the distant smile — when his phone rang.

📞
Unknown number. "Sayo Aizawa. She's been admitted. We found your number in her journal."

Everything went quiet.

Hospital window · Night
Room 307 The hospital smelled like metal and rain.

He found her there — pale, breathing through tubes, IVs lining her arm like spiderwebs.

She wasn't unconscious. But she looked like she was trying very hard not to vanish.

He sat beside her. Didn't speak at first.

Then, softly:
"You promised me time."

Her eyes fluttered. A tired smile.
"…I promised you skies."

"And you're still here," he said, voice breaking. "So keep your promise."

· · ·

The doctor explained it later:
A rare condition. Low survival odds. Surgery was possible — but not guaranteed.

Sayo had declined it before. Said she didn't want to burden anyone.
Said she didn't want to make anyone wait.

But now, someone was waiting.
And he wasn't leaving.

· · ·

That evening, Ren found her journal sitting on the side table.
A new page, unfinished.

📓 From Sayo's Journal Day 64.

He came.
Even when I didn't want him to see me like this. He still came.

Maybe that means I'm not a burden after all.
Maybe it means I'm still allowed to fight.

He gently placed the red ribbon on top of the page.

And whispered:
"Then fight. For me. For you. For us."

The sky outside the hospital window wasn't blue.
But for the first time, it didn't matter.
Because Sayo was still here.

· · ·

Three days passed.
Ren came every single day — sometimes with photos, sometimes with nothing but silence.

Sayo couldn't speak much.
But she listened. Always.

He told her stories.
About the time he tried to bake and nearly set the microwave on fire.
About how he used to be scared of thunder until he realized storms always pass.
About how every photo he'd ever taken meant nothing until he met her.

On the fourth day, she finally whispered:

"I'm scared."

He took her hand.

"So am I."

· · ·

That afternoon, the doctor returned with the final papers.
The surgery was scheduled for next week.

Sayo looked at them for a long time — then at Ren.

"I don't want to disappear while you're holding my hand."

He squeezed it gently.

"Then don't," he said. "Stay. Even if it hurts. Even if it's hard. Just… stay."

On Day 60, Sayo finally smiled like she meant it.

"Thank you," she whispered.
"For what?"
"For giving me a reason to stay."

📓 From Sayo's Journal Day 60.

This time, I didn't write a goodbye.
This time… I wrote a list of things I want to do if I survive.

✨ Things to do if I survive
  1. Return to school.
  2. Sit beside him again.
  3. Let him take a picture of me — just me, fully, openly.
  4. Rewrite the last chapter. Not as the end. But as a beginning.
