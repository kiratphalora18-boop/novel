Case File:
You

The Detective

Mystery Drama Action By AKA

The Symbol · Three Lines · A Triangle · A Bleeding Eye

What a drag…

The thought slipped through Sato Aizawa's mind as he stood at the crime scene, hands in his coat pockets, eyes scanning the lifeless body before him.

At just twenty-four, Sato had already spent two years in the field. Not long enough to be called a veteran—but long enough to know when something wasn't right.

And this case?

It definitely wasn't.

· · ·

Another murder.
Same pattern.
Same method.
And once again… the same symbol.

Three lines.
A triangle.
And at its center—a small, bleeding red eye.

Sato crouched slightly, studying the mark left behind. Whoever was behind this wasn't careless. The scene was spotless—no fingerprints, no signs of struggle, nothing useful.

It was as if the killer erased their own existence.

This makes three…

In just two months, three people had been killed in identical fashion. No connection between victims. No witnesses. No leads.

Only that symbol.

· · ·

"What do you think, Akane?"

Standing beside him was Akane Arima—his senior. With five years of experience, she carried herself with quiet confidence. Not the most seasoned detective, but far from inexperienced.

She folded her arms, her gaze fixed on the symbol.

"There are too many inconsistencies," she said. "But one thing is clear—the pattern isn't random."

Sato glanced at her. "You think it's organized?"

Akane nodded slightly. "I don't think this is the work of a single person. There's a high chance a group is behind it."

"A group…" Sato muttered. "With a signature like this?"

He looked back at the symbol.

"Can we identify it? Maybe link it to a known gang?"

Akane shook her head. "I've already checked. That symbol doesn't match anything in this district's records."

Sato's lips curled faintly.

"Interesting…"

· · ·

Before either of them could continue, footsteps approached.

"Mr. Sato," an officer called out.

Sato turned. "Yes?"

"The chief is requesting your presence. Immediately."

Sato frowned. "Me?"

"You and Ms. Akane."

Sato and Akane exchanged a glance.

That wasn't normal.

The chief never called them both in—especially not for something routine.

· · ·

Sato turned back toward the crime scene one last time. His eyes lingered on the symbol.

Three lines.
A triangle.
A bleeding eye.

For a brief moment… it felt like it was staring back at him.

"Let's go," Akane said.

Sato nodded.

But as he walked away, a strange thought settled in his mind—

This case isn't just another investigation.

It's the beginning of something much bigger.

End of Chapter 1
