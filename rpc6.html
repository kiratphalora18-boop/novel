The Rain Pact
Chapter Six

The Choice

+ Epilogue: After the Rain
☀️

The rain stopped the next morning.
The sky was pale, still streaked with clouds, but the world felt lighter somehow—like it had taken a long breath and finally exhaled.
Aoi stood in front of her mirror, adjusting her uniform. Her fingers paused on the paper crane pinned to her bag.
She smiled, just a little.

At school, she found him before he could find her.
Itsuki blinked in surprise as she stepped in front of his classroom door. "You came early."
"I couldn't sleep," she said.
There was a pause.
Then, she took a breath and asked, "Will you walk with me today?"
Itsuki's smile was softer than ever. "Even if it doesn't rain?"
Aoi looked up at him, the ghost of a grin on her lips.
"Even if it never rains again."

They walked home slowly, side by side. No umbrella, no need. Just sunlight brushing the trees and the faint smell of wet earth lingering in the air.
At the crosswalk, Aoi spoke again.
"I used to hate the rain."
Itsuki turned to her, curious.
"But now… I think I like it," she continued. "Because it gave me something to hold onto when I couldn't hold onto anything else."

Itsuki didn't say anything. He just reached into his bag and pulled out two small, worn paper cranes.
He handed her one.
"I kept it," he said. "Even when I thought I lost you."
Their fingers touched.
They didn't kiss.
They didn't confess in grand words.
They just stood there — two people who had weathered something quiet, something real. And made it out on the other side.
Together.

Epilogue
After the Rain

Sometimes, Aoi still sits by the window when it rains.
She watches droplets chase each other down the glass, listens to the hush it brings.
And somewhere in the distance, she knows Itsuki is smiling too.

They still walk home together — sunny days, cloudy skies, and everything in between.
Because some promises aren't about umbrellas or paper cranes.
They're about choosing each other, again and again.
Even when the skies clear.
Even after the rain.
