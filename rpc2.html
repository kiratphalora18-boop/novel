The Rain Pact
Chapter Two

A Pact Made in Puddles

🌧️

The rain had returned three days in a row now.
Not heavy. Not loud. Just soft, persistent, and oddly comforting.
Aoi found herself standing by the tree again.
She didn't wait for him—at least, that's what she told herself.
But her eyes kept drifting toward the gate.
And sure enough...
"You waited," he said with a grin, twirling the familiar blue umbrella in his hand.
"I didn't," Aoi replied flatly, stepping under it anyway.
He laughed. It was warm, not mocking. "I'm Itsuki, by the way. You never asked."
"Aoi."
"Pretty name."
She looked away. Compliments made her uncomfortable.

They walked in silence again, but it was… peaceful.
The rain pattered gently around them, forming tiny rivers at the edge of the sidewalk.
Aoi kept stealing glances at his hand—his right hand.
The one holding the umbrella.
It stayed steady, tilted slightly more to her side than his.
No one had ever done that for her before.

By the fifth day of rain, it had become routine.
They didn't speak much. Just walked, sharing that little space beneath the umbrella.
But on the sixth day, as they passed a closed flower shop, Itsuki suddenly stopped.
"If it rains again tomorrow…" he said, "Meet me here instead of by the school gate."
"Why?"
He smiled at her.
"Because I want to make a pact."

The rain returned the next morning.
And so did Aoi.
She stood by the flower shop, watching raindrops slide down the glass windows.
A part of her feared he wouldn't show.
That it had been a joke. Or worse—he had grown tired of walking her home.
But then—
"Sorry I'm late."
Itsuki's voice cut through the rain, breathless but bright.

He held out the umbrella again, but didn't open it.
Instead, he held something in his other hand.
Two paper cranes.
"One for me.
One for you," he said. "If it rains, we walk together.
Every time. Deal?"
Aoi hesitated.
Then reached out, accepting the tiny paper bird.
"…Deal."
And just like that, under a closed umbrella and a sky full of clouds, a quiet girl and a strange boy made a promise that neither of them realized would change their lives forever.
