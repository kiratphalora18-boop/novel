✏️ Ten Percent of Forever · Chapter 2

Sketchbook
Secrets

She draws every day. Nobody notices — except him.

Romance Slice of Life By AKA
7:08 AM
🌧 The vending machine overhang

She was there before him. For once.

The next morning, she was there before him.

Hiro almost thought he was dreaming. He rubbed his eyes twice, even peeked around the corner to double-check the time.

7:08 AM.

Mei stood beneath the vending machine's overhang, hugging her sketchbook to her chest, rain still lingering in the air from last night's storm.
Her hair was slightly damp, curled in places it usually wasn't.

She didn't look at him when he walked up beside her.

"…Morning," Hiro said.

She gave the faintest nod. "Hey."

A pause. Then:

"You're usually here first."
"You're usually wearing earbuds," he countered.

She blinked. Her hand twitched toward her bag like she'd forgotten something.
"…Left them at home."

For a few moments, they stood in silence, the sound of dripping from the vending machine filling the space between them.

· · ·

Then Hiro glanced down.

Mei was sketching something — fast, light lines, barely even looking at the page. Her hands moved with practiced motion, like the drawing wasn't being made, just revealed.

"You're drawing?" he asked, leaning slightly.

She froze.
Closed the sketchbook in a swift motion.
Hugged it close again.

"…Sorry," Hiro said, stepping back. "Wasn't trying to peek."

"It's not that," Mei murmured, eyes fixed on the ground.
"Just… I don't usually let people see."

"Even though you draw every day?"

She gave a soft shrug. "People don't notice."

"I noticed."

That made her look at him. Really look.

"…You're weird," she said quietly.

Hiro smiled. "I get that a lot."

They both laughed — softly, but it felt loud for two people who never really talked before yesterday.

Then the bus rolled by. Its doors creaked open.
They stepped on, still side by side.

· · ·

In class, they didn't sit together. Mei had her spot by the window, headphones in.
Hiro was closer to the back with his usual friends, who were too busy chatting about a new game to notice anything different.

But when he opened his notebook later, he found something tucked inside.

A tiny, folded slip of paper. No name. No note.

✉️ A folded slip of paper — no name, no note

Just a pencil sketch.

✏️ What she drew

The vending machine.
Two silhouettes under the rain.
And one umbrella.

She noticed him too.
End of Chapter 2
