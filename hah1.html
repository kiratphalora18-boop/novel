Him and Her
Chapter One

Her

✿

I am Kazuki.
An average high schooler.
Second year.
I have always been… nobody.
Not because people ignore me.
Because I prefer it that way.
Being average keeps attention away from me.
Right now I am sitting in my usual place, the window seat of our classroom.
From here I can see the school yard.
There stands a sakura tree.
Strangely, it is blooming even though it is not the season for it.
Soft pink petals move with the wind and sometimes drift toward the classroom window.
I do not know why, but looking at that tree always relaxes me.

And then…
There is her.
Sayori.
Calm. Beautiful.
The kind of person who naturally becomes the center of attention without even trying.
Students often gather around her desk during breaks.
Some ask questions.
Some just want to talk.
She smiles politely and answers them.
Watching that scene always makes me feel like she belongs to a completely different world.
Sometimes I wonder…
Does she actually like all that attention?
Or does she just accept it because she has to?
I guess I will never know.

The truth is, I have rarely talked to her.
Actually… only once.
During the opening ceremony.
It was just a small conversation.
Nothing important.
But for some reason I still remember it.
It is not like I have no friends.
I do talk to a few people in class.
But sometimes I wonder…
Do they really want me around?
Or am I just someone who happens to be there?
I guess I will never know that either.

A small breeze enters the classroom through the window.
A sakura petal floats inside and lands softly on my desk.
I look outside again.
Sayori is laughing with her friends near the front of the class.
And strangely…
Just looking at her somehow calms my mind.
Sometimes a thought crosses my mind.
A simple one.
What if… we were friends?

✿   ·   ✿
Kazuki — POV

Yep. It is that time of the year again.
The time when our school decides to change the seating arrangement.
Honestly, I hate this rule.
It means I might have to give up my window seat and sit somewhere random.
The teacher stands in front of the class with a box full of numbers.
One by one, students walk up and pick a number that decides their seat.
My turn comes.
I walk to the desk and pick a folded paper.
I open it.
9.
I look at the seating chart on the board.
Good.
Just one seat behind my usual window seat.
That is not bad at all.
I quietly sit down.

Then I hear someone talking near the front.
"Sayori, what number did you get?"
Sayori: "Mine is 17."
"Seventeen? That is the last seat in the second row."
I look at the seating chart again.
Wait.
The last seat in the second row means...
That seat is right next to mine.
That was unexpected.

A few seconds later, Sayori starts walking toward her new seat.
Some students are still talking to her, but she just laughs lightly and moves past them.
Then she stops beside my desk.
For a moment, I do not know what to say.
She speaks first.
Sayori: "Hello. Nice to meet you. I will be sitting next to you."
Kazuki: "H-hello… same here."
Great.
Why did I fumble that badly?
Sayori smiles.
Without thinking, I smile too.
Sayori: "You are Kazuki, right?"
Kazuki: "Yes… I am."
She nods slightly.
Sayori: "Then let's be friends."

Friends?
That word catches me off guard.
Kazuki: "You… want to be friends?"
Sayori tilts her head a little.
Sayori: "You don't want to?"
Kazuki: "Oh, no. I mean… yes. Sure."
She laughs softly.
And just like that…
The distance between our worlds became a little smaller.

— End of Chapter One —
