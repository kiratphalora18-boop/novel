🎭 Ten Percent of Forever · Chapter 5

The Confession
Game

She said something. He heard the one she didn't.

Romance Slice of Life By AKA

"Truth or dare?"

It was late. The class trip was on its final night, and everyone was gathered in a messy circle of pillows and snack wrappers, their teacher long asleep and totally unaware.

The lights were dimmed. Someone had brought a flashlight and aimed it at the ceiling, casting shadows that danced across the paper walls.

Hiro sat at the edge of the circle. Mei was across from him, her knees drawn up to her chest, hoodie sleeves covering half her fingers.
She didn't usually join games like these.
But tonight — for whatever reason — she stayed.

"Who do you like?"

"Your turn, Mei. Truth or dare?"

Mei looked up, blinking. The room quieted. Even the most restless boys leaned in.
"…Truth," she said softly.

Haruka smirked. "Who do you like?"

A collective ooooohhh spread around the room.

Mei froze. Hiro straightened, suddenly too aware of everything — the room, the air, the way his hands were clenched without realizing it.

Mei opened her mouth. Then paused.
And smiled.
The smallest, most mischievous smile he had ever seen on her.

"…You," she said, pointing at Haruka.

Laughter exploded across the circle. Even Haruka blushed.
Someone shouted, "She's totally bluffing!"
Another voice: "She didn't even deny it though!"

Mei leaned back into the shadows, pulling her hoodie over her head slightly.

But Hiro noticed the way her eyes flicked to him — only for a second.
Like she was asking: did you hear what I didn't say?

He met her gaze.
And nodded.

💬 The class group chat

"Nah, she's just a quiet kid. Not really my type lol."

He meant it as sarcasm. Mei had seen it.

And she stopped talking to him the next day.

It was subtle at first.

🕊 What disappeared
  • —No umbrella waiting under the vending machine roof.
  • —No sketchbook left open on the bench.
  • —No rice ball at lunch.
  • —No soft glances in the hallway.
  • —Just empty spaces where she used to be.

Hiro noticed it like a missing breath.

When he finally approached her after class, her eyes didn't meet his.
"Mei — wait, can we talk?"
"I'm busy," she said, without looking up.

"Is this about that message? Because that wasn't—"

She stood up, gathering her things. Her voice was quiet, but her words hit harder than shouting.
"You didn't even try to explain."
"I didn't know you saw—"
"Exactly. You didn't know. You didn't ask. You just said something and left me to feel stupid about it."

"I'm sorry. I didn't mean it like that, I—"
"I don't care what you meant."

That stopped him cold.

Mei hugged her sketchbook to her chest like a shield. Her eyes flicked up once, just enough for him to see the hurt behind them.

"Just… don't say things you don't mean," she whispered. "Especially not about people who take you seriously."

Then she walked away.

· · ·

The days that followed were quiet again.
But not the comforting kind.

It was the old kind — the kind Hiro used to enjoy before he knew what it meant to miss someone.

She was still in class. Still drawing. Still passing him in the hallway.
But the sketchbook never opened when he was around.
And the 7:10 AM morning silence now felt like punishment.
