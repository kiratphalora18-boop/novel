The Petal That Falls With a Smile
Chapter One

Sakura in November

🌸

The sakura tree wasn't supposed to bloom in November.
It stood there anyway—bare, crooked, and stubborn. Haruki liked that about it.
Everyone walked past it, eyes glued to their phones, heads buried in winter scarves.
But he noticed. It was the only thing in school that didn't pretend to be something it wasn't.
That day, he wasn't planning on lingering. Just drop off the class printouts, then run to cram school.
But when he turned the corner behind the old library, he saw her.
Sitting beneath the tree.
Knees to her chest. Face hidden.
And shoulders trembling.

He blinked.
Sora.
She wasn't the type to cry in public.
Actually, she wasn't the type to talk much at all. Quiet, always alone during lunch.
People said she transferred last year from some school up north.
Said she didn't have friends. Said she liked it that way.
Haruki didn't believe rumors.
He took a cautious step forward, rustling the fallen leaves.
She froze.
"…Sorry," he said quickly, raising his hands like he was caught stealing.
"Didn't mean to interrupt. I just…"
He paused, unsure what he was even doing there.
"…You okay?"

She didn't look up. Just wiped her face with her sleeve and stood.
"No one ever comes here," she murmured.
Her voice was softer than he expected. And tired.
"I do," he said. "Every Tuesday and Friday."
He pointed to the printouts in his hand.
"Student council delivery boy."
That got her to smile. Barely. Just a twitch at the corner of her lips.
"Then I guess this spot's yours," she said.
"No," Haruki shook his head. "Let's share."
For a second, silence fell between them like snow.

Then she nodded—just once—and turned to leave.
"Wait," he called out, without thinking.
"…I'm Haruki."
She paused, looking at him properly now. Her eyes were red, but clear.
"Sora," she said.
Then she walked off, leaving the faintest trace of sakura scent in the cold November air.
