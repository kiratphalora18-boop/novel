🌧 The Day She Stayed · Chapter 6

The Sky That
Stood Still

He waited. Nine hours. One letter.

Romance Drama Sad By AKA

It was raining again.

The kind of rain that felt like silence. No thunder. No drama. Just droplets tapping against the hospital window like a ticking clock.

Sayo was prepped for surgery.

Ren sat beside her, pretending his hands weren't shaking.

She looked at him — eyes heavy, face calm.
"Will you be here when I wake up?"

He reached into his bag and pulled out a letter — sealed with a piece of red ribbon.

"I'll be here. But if I'm not… open this."

Her fingers hovered over it. Then pulled back.
"Only if you're not?"
He nodded.
"And only then."

She smiled.
"You're always giving me rules to break."

7:03 AM They wheeled her away.

Ren stood in the hallway long after the doors closed.

His heart didn't beat right. His thoughts were noise.

He didn't cry.

Instead, he opened his camera, found a photo from the week before — a blurry one of her laughing, wind in her hair, sunlight dancing on her skin.

He stared at it like it was holding her soul.
Then he whispered:

"Please stay."

📷
Blurry. Wind. Laughing. The one that held her soul.
9 Hours · He waited the entire time

A chair too cold. A room too white. A letter he never wanted her to read.

He thought about opening it himself.
Just in case.

But he didn't.

Because if he did… it meant he'd given up.

· · ·

Then the doctor appeared.
Tired. Calm.

"She's stable," he said.

Ren didn't move.

It took ten full seconds for the words to reach him.

And when they did — he ran.

· · ·

She was pale. Hooked up to wires. Eyes closed.

But breathing.
Alive.

He sat beside her. Held her hand. Laid the letter beside her pillow — unopened.

"You're not allowed to read that anymore," he whispered.

The Things We Don't Say
(But Mean Anyway)

It took her two days to fully wake up.

When she opened her eyes, the room was empty — except for the photo pinned to the ceiling above her bed.

A picture of the sky.

Not the perfect blue she once feared.
But a cloudy, broken one — the kind that looked like it had survived something.

📷 Pinned to the ceiling above her bed

Not perfect. Broken. The kind that had survived something.

Beside her was the letter Ren had given her.
Still sealed.
Still waiting.

She didn't open it.
She didn't have to.

He came in that afternoon with a plastic bag full of vending machine snacks and a face that looked like he hadn't slept in a week.

She smiled.
"You look like you lost a fight with a raccoon."

He dropped the snacks, sat beside her, and exhaled like he'd been holding his breath since the moment she left.

"You're here," he said.
"I told you I'd stay."

He didn't cry.
But his voice cracked when he whispered:
"I didn't open your letter."

She reached for it. Placed it in his hands.
"Then let's burn it together."

He blinked. "Wait — what?"
"It's not needed anymore. You're here. I'm here. Let's make new rules."

He stared at her.
Then laughed.
A real laugh. The kind that didn't carry weight, just relief.

🔥 That evening · Outside · Wind gentle · Sun low

They held both letters over the flame — hers and his.
Watched as the papers curled, burned, disappeared.

💬 What they said
Sayo "I never read yours."
Ren "I never read yours either."
Both "Good."

"Why?"

She looked at him.
"Because if I had… I think I would've broken again. And I don't want to break anymore."

· · ·

That night, he asked:
"What happens now?"

She answered:
"We live. Clumsily. Softly. One picture at a time."

We don't talk about the surgery much.

Because he knows. And I know.

We're not counting down anymore.
We're counting up.
