Letters in a Cold Spring

Maybe some goodbyes want to be found.

Drama Romance Slice of Life By AKA

The third letter came three days later.

Nao found herself checking the bench more than she meant to — on the way to class, during lunch, even once after sunset when the campus was nearly empty.

She told herself it was just curiosity.

Not hope.

Never hope.

That Friday, she slipped her fingers beneath the floorboard again.

The book was still there.

And so was a reply.

She unfolded it carefully, eyes scanning the lines like they might vanish if she blinked.

✉ Letter from H

I knew her.
Or… I thought I did.

Sora wasn't the kind of girl you forget.
But she tried hard to be forgotten.

She left behind a lot of silence.

That letter was mine. I wrote it two years ago.
I never really planned for anyone to see it.

But I'm glad someone did.

You asked if it was waiting.
Maybe.
Maybe some goodbyes want to be found.

— H

Nao stared at the signature.

H.

Just a single letter. But it gave shape to the mystery — a boy who once waited here, loved someone who couldn't stay, and left pieces of himself behind like petals on the wind.

She pressed the note to her chest for a second, then pulled out her own paper.

This one came slower. Her pen paused more often.

✉ Letter from N

I transferred here last month.
I didn't come with much. Not even a goodbye.

People talk about starting over like it's brave.
But sometimes, it just feels like running.

I think I ran.
From someone I loved. From something I wasn't ready to face.

So maybe this is what we have in common.
You waited.
I didn't.

But somehow we both ended up under the same tree.

— N

She folded it gently, slid it back between the pages, and let the book rest in its hidden home.

As she stood up, the wind passed through again — softer this time.

And though the branches above were still bare, Nao thought she saw the smallest pink bud beginning to form.

Spring was coming.

Quietly.

🌸
The First Petal Falls
You feel like a voice I didn't know I missed.

The first petal appeared on a Wednesday.

Nao almost missed it — half-asleep on her way to school, earbuds in, the usual fog of morning wrapped around her thoughts.

But as she passed the old library and turned toward the bench, something soft drifted down in front of her face.

She caught it without thinking.

A sakura petal.

The first of the season.

It was barely pink — more memory than color — and it rested in her palm like it had traveled a long way to get there.

She looked up. The tree was still mostly bare, branches reaching like fingers against the sky. But there it was. One stubborn blossom, high up and trembling in the wind.

She smiled, without meaning to.

Then she sat, and reached beneath the floorboard.

The book was there.

And so was a new letter.

Her heart beat faster as she unfolded it.

✉ Letter from H

N,

I think about her less now.
That feels like a betrayal, somehow.
But also like healing.

I waited for Sora because I thought she needed me to.
But maybe I needed to wait more than she needed to be waited for.

It's strange. You've written more to me than most people have spoken to me this year.

Thank you.
You feel like a voice I didn't know I missed.

— H

Nao reread it three times.

That last line sat with her the longest.

You feel like a voice I didn't know I missed.

Her chest ached in a way that wasn't quite sadness. Something gentler. Like recognition.

And for the first time, she let herself wonder who he was.

Who H was now. Not the boy from the letter to Sora. Not the past. But the one writing back to her now — this quiet connection unfolding between two people who hadn't yet met.

Or had they?

She looked around the courtyard. Students passed by, laughing, shouting, shoulders bumping in that clumsy way high schoolers move. No one seemed to notice her.

But for a second — just a second — she thought she saw a boy across the yard, standing near the vending machines, his eyes flicking toward the tree.

And then away.

He didn't smile. Didn't wave.

But her heart stirred anyway.

Maybe.

Maybe not.

📖
She wrote back. Beneath the cherry tree · The hidden book
✉ Letter from N

I don't know who you are.
But I think I'd like to.

Only if you want that too.

— N

She tucked the note in, sealed the paperback, and pressed the floorboard shut.

As she stood, another petal fell.

And she didn't feel like a stranger anymore.
