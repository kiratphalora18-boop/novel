Ten Percent of Forever
Chapter One

The Umbrella Incident

10%

Rain didn't fall — it whispered.
It whispered across the empty sidewalk.
It tapped gently on the school noticeboard. It slid lazily down the side of the vending machine where Hiro was already waiting, hunched over with his hoodie pulled up, hands tucked inside his blazer sleeves.
He always arrived too early.
Not because he was desperate to be at school, but because the silence before the noise began was the only part of the day that felt... still.
And then she passed by.
Same time. Same steps. Same oversized sketchbook clutched to her chest like a shield.
Mei.
He didn't know much about her.
Just that she always had earbuds in, and her hair was the kind of messy that probably wasn't accidental.
She never looked at anyone. Not really. Not even him, even though they passed each other nearly every day.

But today — something changed.
She stopped. Just slightly.
Because today, the rain wasn't gentle.
It fell harder. Sudden. Sharp.
And Mei, caught in the middle of the street, stared up in surprise — no umbrella, no hood, no chance to make it inside dry.
Their eyes met for the first time.
Hiro blinked. She blinked back.
"...You can wait here," he said, scooting to the side under the vending machine's metal overhang.
She hesitated. Looked around like there might be a better option. There wasn't.
"...Thanks."
She stepped under, careful not to stand too close.
The overhang was barely big enough for two.
Her shoulder brushed his arm.
He tried not to move. Tried not to breathe too loud.

Mei pulled her earbuds out slowly.
Rain filled the silence between them.
After a few seconds, Hiro chuckled, awkward.
"You know… you pass me every day."
Her gaze flicked toward him, cautious.
"You notice?"
"You hum when you walk," he said, then immediately regretted it.
"Not in a weird way! Just… it's kinda nice."
Mei looked away, but he could swear her lips twitched.
She didn't answer.
Another gust of wind pushed the rain sideways.
She flinched, pressing the sketchbook tighter to her chest.
Hiro shifted his bag.
"…I have an umbrella," he said.
She glanced at him again. This time longer.
He held it out between them.
"We're headed the same way, right?"

She didn't answer.
Just stared at the handle, then at him.
"…Don't worry. I'll hold it awkwardly so you don't get wet," he added, trying to make it light.
Finally, she took it from his hand.
Slowly. Like she wasn't sure if this was a joke.
"Okay," she said quietly. "But I hold it."
He blinked. "…Why?"
"Because you're tall. I'll stay dry. You'll get wet."
She said it plainly, like it was a fact.
Then added, almost too soft to hear, "It's fair."
They walked the rest of the way to school like that —
sharing the umbrella, sharing silence, sharing something that neither of them had a name for yet.

— End of Chapter One —
