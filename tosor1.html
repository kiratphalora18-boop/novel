The Other Side of Rain
Chapter One

The Girl Who Always Forgot Her Umbrella

🌧️

Minato hated attention.
So when he saw her again — soaked, shivering, balancing two textbooks over her head like they'd help — he looked away.
The girl was always forgetting her umbrella.
Not once. Not twice.
This was the fourth time in two weeks.
He should've kept walking.
But his feet moved before his brain could argue.
"Here," he said, holding out his umbrella.
She blinked. "What?"
"You're always forgetting it. Just take mine."

She looked at the umbrella, then back at him. "You're… Minato, right? Class 2-C?"
"…Yeah."
"I'm Rika."
He knew.
Everyone knew Rika — loud, late to class, always laughing with others.
But today, she didn't laugh.
She just stared at the umbrella like it was something unfamiliar.
Like kindness confused her.
"Thanks," she whispered, finally taking it.
Minato turned to leave.
Then—
"…Can I return it to you tomorrow?"
He paused. "Yeah. Sure."
She didn't know it yet.
But that small question was the first drop of her own storm.

· · ·

Minato didn't expect her to show up.
He walked to school alone the next morning, his hands in his pockets, head down. The sky was gray, but the rain hadn't started. Yet. The air carried that heavy stillness, like clouds were waiting to burst.
At the school gates, he paused.
And there she was.
Rika.
Holding his umbrella with both hands like it might break if she wasn't careful.
"You came," he said, surprised.
She shrugged. "You trusted me with your umbrella. Least I could do."
He reached for it, but she hesitated.
"Actually…" she said. "Can we share it? It's probably going to rain on the way back. And I… kinda liked yesterday."
Minato stared at her for a moment.
Rika — the loud girl, the forgetful one, the one everyone noticed — looked almost shy.
"…Okay," he said quietly.

They walked home together under the same umbrella later that day.
It did rain, just as she predicted — a slow drizzle, soft enough to whisper but heavy enough to need cover.
They didn't talk much.
But Minato noticed something strange.
She wasn't loud when she walked with him.
She didn't crack jokes.
She didn't fidget.
She just… existed beside him.
Like she'd been waiting for someone who wouldn't ask her to perform.

As they neared the turn where most students split off, they passed a quiet street — and Minato paused.
There, under the cherry tree near the school wall, stood a familiar pair.
A boy and girl.
Aoi and Itsuki.
Sharing an umbrella. Talking softly. Paper cranes in hand.
Rika stopped too. She watched them, her expression unreadable.
"…I see them sometimes," she said. "They look like they belong in a different world."
Minato nodded. "They made a pact."
Rika turned to him. "What kind of pact?"
He looked at her, and this time… he didn't look away.
"One that started with an umbrella too."

— End of Chapter One —
