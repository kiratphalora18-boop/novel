The Petal That Falls With a Smile
Chapter Two

The Wrong Kind Of Smile

🌧️

Haruki wasn’t sure why he kept looking for her.
Maybe it was the way she said his name—soft like she hadn’t spoken to anyone in days. Or maybe it was just that she didn’t run away when she cried. She didn’t apologize for being broken. He found her again two days later.
Same spot. Same crooked sakura tree.
This time, she was reading. A thick paperback with a worn spine and no cover.
He didn’t say anything. Just dropped the printouts by the council box and sat down on the opposite end of the bench. Ten minutes passed in silence.

Then she spoke.
“You don’t talk much for someone who talks too much in class.” Haruki laughed, surprised. “You noticed?”
“I sit two rows behind you. It’s hard not to.”
He grinned. “You never laugh, though. Not even at my best material.”
“That’s because your best material is awful.”
He clutched his chest dramatically. “Ouch.”
And then—
She smiled. Just a little.
Not a polite smile. Not the fake one people give to make conversations end faster. It was the kind that cracked something open. The kind that said: I haven’t done this in a while. It hit Haruki harder than any punchline ever had.
“You should smile more,” he said, honest without meaning to be.

She looked away.
“You shouldn’t say things you don’t mean.”
“I mean it,” he said. “When you smiled just now—it didn’t look like it belonged to you. But I hope one day it does.” Sora blinked. She stared at him for a long time. Then slowly closed her book. “I used to smile a lot,” she whispered. “Before.”
“Before…?”
She didn’t answer. Just stood up and dusted off her skirt.
“I’ll be here again tomorrow,” she said.
And that was it.
She was gone again. But this time, Haruki didn’t feel like she was running away. He felt like she was asking him to come back.

🌸   ·   🌸

By the third meeting, the silence between them had become comfortable.
Haruki brought melon bread and two cans of coffee from the vending machine.
Sora didn’t say thank you, but she took the can and opened it with a soft click.
That was enough.
They talked about nothing — Teachers they didn’t like. Books she’d read.
Stupid pranks Haruki pulled in middle school.
And that time a pigeon flew into the math class window and the whole room screamed.

It felt easy. Natural.
Like they’d always known each other.
But then, as they watched the gray sky turn gold, Sora asked,
“Do you believe people change?”
Haruki tilted his head. “What kind of change?” “Like... if someone used to be kind. Then one day they just stop.”
He thought for a moment.
“Yeah,” he said. “But I think they have reasons. Even if they can’t say them.”
She looked down at her hands. Her fingers were trembling.
“…What if that reason hurts someone else?”

Haruki hesitated.
“…Then I hope the other person waits long enough to understand.”
For a long time, Sora didn’t respond.
Just breathed. Slowly. Carefully. Like each word she didn’t say was being held back.
Then, out of nowhere, she said: “I pushed someone away last year. On purpose.”
He looked at her.
She still wasn’t crying. But her eyes had that same storm from the first day.
“I told him I didn’t care. That we were just kids. That it didn’t matter.”
Haruki’s heart sank. Not from jealousy.
From the way she said it — like it haunted her. Like every word was a nail she’d hammered into her own chest.
“Did it matter?” he asked softly.
“…More than anything,” she whispered.
The wind rustled the sakura branches above. A single petal — somehow — drifted down between them. A memory out of season.
He didn’t ask who the boy was.
Didn’t ask what happened.

Instead, he stood. Gently pulled his scarf loose. And wrapped it around her shoulders.
“It’s getting cold,” he said. “You’ll catch a cold, Sora.”
She looked up at him — startled. Vulnerable.
And then she nodded, without a word.

— End of Chapter Two —
