🍂 Ten Percent of Forever · Chapter 4

The Sound
of Her Laugh

The exact opposite of melancholy.

Romance Slice of Life By AKA

It started with a whisper.

"I have to read in front of the class tomorrow," Mei said, barely audible over the rustling trees as they walked down the path behind the school.

They often met here now, in the ten minutes before homeroom — a quiet space where the city faded and time softened.

Hiro blinked. "You? Reading aloud?"
She nodded. "My turn. Literature presentation."
"But you hate speaking."
"I don't hate it," she said. "I'm just… bad at it."
"I've never even heard you raise your voice."
"Exactly," she mumbled.

They stopped under a tree. Leaves fell lazily, spinning in the soft wind.
Mei looked nervous. She clutched a crumpled sheet of paper in her hand.

"…Want help?" Hiro offered.
She hesitated. Then handed him the paper.
"Don't laugh."
"I won't."

They sat on the bench. She cleared her throat. Tried once. Stumbled. Tried again. Tripped over the word "melancholy."

"Melon what?" Hiro teased.
She glared.
"You said you wouldn't laugh."
"I'm not. Just offering… creative pronunciation."

She pouted — and tried again. He read along beside her, echoing the tricky words until she started to find the rhythm.
It took four tries.
By the fifth, she didn't pause.

📖 Her fifth try

"And though the autumn leaves may fall in silence, their echo lingers in the heart of those who watched them go."

She didn't pause once.

Hiro clapped once, dramatic.
She rolled her eyes — but the corner of her mouth twitched.
Then he added, dead serious: "Honestly? You sound cooler than the author."

She stared.

And then — for the first time in all the mornings, all the lunches, all the quiet walks between classes —

She laughed.

✨ That laugh

It wasn't loud.
It wasn't perfect.
But it was real. And soft. And bright.
The kind of laugh that started small and surprised even her.

Hiro blinked.
"…Wow," he said.
"What?"
"That was the first time I've heard your laugh."

She immediately went quiet.
"Don't make it weird," she muttered.
"I'm not!" he grinned. "Just saying… I like it."

She didn't answer, but her cheeks turned a soft pink. She looked down, letting her hair fall in front of her face.

He didn't push it.
They just sat there, the paper still in her hands, sunlight filtering through the trees.

And Hiro thought: if her laugh were a sound, it would be the exact opposite of melancholy.

· · ·

The mountains were quieter than anyone expected.

By night, the class trip felt less like a field trip and more like a dream — the air crisp, the lights dimmed, every voice muffled beneath thick layers of sleep and moonlight.

Their inn was traditional. Tatami floors, futons, low ceilings, creaky hallways.

But Hiro couldn't sleep.
So he wandered outside, hoodie zipped, hands in his pockets.

And found her — again — before he even tried.

Mei sat alone on the wooden deck behind the inn, legs swinging gently, sketchbook beside her and her eyes on the sky.

"You always find the quietest spots," he said softly.
She turned her head. "And you always find me in them."

"Can't sleep?"
"No. Too many voices in my head."
"Want me to chase them out?"
"That depends. Will you do it dramatically?"

He cleared his throat and waved his arms toward the stars.
"Be gone, anxiety demons! Mei commands it!"

She burst into a laugh — softer than the one under the trees, but warmer somehow.

🌌 The inn rooftop · Class trip night

"A thousand stars scattered like forgotten wishes."

"Did you draw this?" Hiro asked, gesturing upward.
She shook her head. "Not yet. I don't know how to draw something this… big."

They sat in silence for a long time after that.

Then she said, almost too quiet to hear:
"…I don't believe in forever."

He turned toward her, surprised.
"I mean… nothing lasts. Friendships. People. Feelings. Even memories fade."

Hiro looked up again. Then back at her.
"Maybe. But even if something doesn't last forever… doesn't mean it wasn't real."

She didn't reply.
So he added, with a quiet smile:

💬 The exchange
Hiro "Then just give me ten percent of forever."
Mei "What if I already am?"

She looked at him.
Really looked.
Like something inside her stopped holding back for a second.

And just like that, the stars above them didn't feel so distant anymore.
