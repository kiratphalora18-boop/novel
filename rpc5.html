The Rain Pact
Chapter Five

Umbrellas Left Behind

🌂

The clouds had cleared for days now.
Sunlight painted golden stripes across classroom floors, and a warm breeze made the curtains dance. For once, students didn't talk about the weather—they talked about the upcoming School Cultural Festival.
Aoi didn't usually care for these things. She avoided crowds, noise, and events where she might be noticed.
But when the teacher assigned her to the literature booth, she didn't argue. She just nodded and wrote her name on the sheet.
That's when she saw him—Itsuki—laughing with someone else.
A girl from Class 3-C. Short hair, bold eyes, and the kind of smile that didn't hesitate.
They stood close, like they knew each other well. The girl handed him a flyer, and he grinned.
Aoi wasn't the jealous type.
But she felt something. A quiet sting in her chest.
She turned away.

Later that day, she found herself under the cherry tree again.
No rain. No umbrella. Just the shadow of a habit.
"Waiting for rain?" a voice teased.
Itsuki stood beside her, hands in his pockets.
"No," she replied, a little sharper than she meant to.
He blinked. "Did I do something?"
Aoi hesitated. Then shook her head. "Forget it."
But he didn't.

On the day of the festival, the school was alive with color. Stalls, music, lights. Aoi wore her usual uniform, sleeves rolled up, helping arrange books and handwritten poetry cards at the booth.
At the far end of the hallway, she spotted them again—Itsuki and the girl. They were setting up the photography exhibit together. The girl tugged playfully at his sleeve, pointing at a picture.
And Itsuki smiled.
That same smile he used to give her under the umbrella.
Aoi looked down at the paper crane she had taped to the corner of her booth.
It felt… distant now. Like a dream fading in sunlight.
She didn't wait by the tree that day.
And for the first time since the pact was made,
she brought an umbrella…
but didn't open it.

· · ·

It rained that evening.
Not a thunderstorm. Just a soft, cold drizzle, like the sky was breathing gently. The kind of rain that once felt like comfort.
But this time, Aoi didn't walk to the tree.
She sat by her window, the blue umbrella leaning against her wall, untouched.
Across her desk, the paper crane stared at her. Faded now. The corners curled slightly. It looked more fragile than she remembered.
Aoi rested her chin on her knees.
"I don't know why it hurts," she whispered, "but it does."

At school, she and Itsuki still passed each other.
Sometimes, he waved. Sometimes, she looked away.
He didn't ask what was wrong again. Maybe he sensed she didn't want to talk. Or maybe… he was hurting too.
They didn't walk together.
Not for days.
The umbrella stayed folded. The pact, it seemed, had been left behind in sunlight.

One afternoon, as rain pattered outside, Aoi went to the old music room. Dust still clung to the piano. The same two chairs still sat side by side.
She sat alone.
The silence wasn't peaceful anymore. It echoed.
Then the door creaked.
Aoi turned—expecting a teacher, a classmate, maybe no one at all.
But it was Itsuki.
Soaked, breathless, eyes searching.
"I waited by the tree," he said. "You didn't come."
Aoi looked away. "You don't have to wait anymore."
He stepped closer. "But I want to."

Silence.
Rain drummed on the windows, soft but insistent.
"I saw you," Aoi said at last. "With her. You looked happy."
"She's just an old friend," he said. "But even if I smiled… it wasn't the same."
She looked up.
"I didn't understand it until you stopped walking with me," he continued. "It wasn't the rain I liked. It was you."
Aoi felt the tears before they fell.
Slow. Silent. Like the rain they always shared.
Itsuki sat beside her—close, but not too close.
No umbrella. No crane.
Just two people, learning how to speak without running from the quiet.
