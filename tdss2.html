📷 The Day She Stayed · Chapter 2

The Rule

No questions. No photos. No falling.

Romance Drama Sad By AKA

"Why don't you ever look up?"
Ren asked without looking at her.
It was lunch break.

Sayo sat beside him on the rooftop, feet dangling over the edge, hands gripping her journal so tight her knuckles turned pale. The wind was soft. The kind of wind that made the sky feel too far away to reach.

She didn't answer immediately.
Instead, she stared straight ahead, eyes unfocused, voice barely above a whisper.

"…Because if I start looking up," she said, "I won't want to come back down."

Ren glanced at her, camera resting on his lap.

"Maybe that's not a bad thing."

Sayo smiled — the kind of smile that doesn't reach the eyes.
Like a habit she was trying to forget.
Then, for the first time, she turned to him and said:

"Let's set some rules."

📜 The Rules
  • I No questions about the journal.
  • II No photographs of each other.
  • III And no falling in love.

He stared at her.
Confused. A little amused. Mostly curious.

"You think I'm going to fall for you?" he asked.

She looked at him — really looked at him — and said nothing.

That was the first time Ren noticed the sadness behind her eyes wasn't just sadness. It was something deeper. Like her soul had already started packing its bags to leave.

That night, Ren looked through his sky folder again.
617 photos.
But not a single one made him feel like that rooftop conversation did.

So he broke the rule.

And took a picture of her — just her silhouette, leaning forward as the wind tugged at her sleeves and hair, head tilted like she was listening to a song no one else could hear.

"Like the sky couldn't cry loud enough,
so it settled for a whisper." The day she didn't come

It started raining that morning.

Not the kind that lashes at windows or floods shoes — but the gentle, lingering kind. Like the sky couldn't cry loud enough, so it settled for a whisper.

Sayo didn't show up to school.
No one seemed to notice, except Ren.

He kept glancing at her empty chair, checking the time, checking again. She had become part of his routine without asking. Like the quiet in between camera clicks.

That evening, he stood in the usual spot — rooftop, camera in hand, waiting.
She didn't come.

The next day, she returned, soaking wet, ribbon missing.

"You're late," he said.

She sat beside him without a word. Her journal was clutched tighter than usual. Her eyes red.
Ren didn't ask. He wanted to. But he remembered the rule.

So instead, he handed her something.

A photo — printed from his camera — of the cloudy sky that rained the day she didn't come.

She blinked.
"What is this?"
Ren smiled, small. "The sky missed you."

For a moment, Sayo just stared.
Then she looked away quickly, pressing the photo to her chest like it might vanish.

"…Don't do that," she whispered.
"Do what?"
"Make me wish I had more time."

· · ·

They didn't talk much after that. But they sat together every lunch.
He'd photograph clouds. She'd sketch things in her journal. Once, he saw a drawing — of a boy with a camera staring at a sky filled with tiny stars.

She noticed him looking and shut it instantly.

That night, Ren took another photo.
But this time, he didn't name it by time.
