🔍 Case File: You · Chapter 3

The Interrogation

Fear. Confusion. Shock. Or a very good liar.

Mystery Drama Action By AKA

Sato grabbed his coat from the back of the chair.

"Akane, we're leaving shortly. You ready?"

Akane looked up from her desk. "Yeah, just give me a second."

A few moments later, both detectives stepped out of the agency building and headed toward the address listed in the report.

RIE's residence.

The evening sky was gray, covered in heavy clouds as the city lights slowly began to flicker awake.

As Sato drove, he kept thinking about the DNA report.

"If RIE really is the killer…" he muttered, eyes fixed on the road, "is the DNA sample enough to prove it?"

Akane sat beside him quietly before answering.

"It's strong evidence. But for a murder case? We need more than that."

Sato sighed softly. "Yeah… figured."

🏢
Destination: RIE's Residence Modern Apartment · Arrived ~30 min later

About thirty minutes later, they arrived outside a modern apartment building.

After confirming the room number, Sato pressed the doorbell.

A few seconds later, the door opened.

A young man stood there, slightly confused but calm.

"Hello… and you are?"

Sato showed his ID.

"Sato Aizawa. This is Akane Arima. We're with the Detective Agency."

Akane gave a small nod. "We'd like to ask you a few questions, if that's alright."

The man blinked once before stepping aside. "Me? Sure… please come in."

Person of Interest
RIE

Alibi Client — Ruby Zeni
Apartment Neat. Too normal.

The apartment was surprisingly neat.

Minimal furniture. Clean floors. No strange smell.

Too normal.

Sato's eyes quietly scanned the room as they sat down.

"You're RIE, correct?"

"Yes," the man replied calmly. "Please, have a seat."

Sato leaned forward slightly. "No need for formalities. Let's get straight to the point."

The atmosphere immediately became heavier.

Sato opened his notebook. "Where were you on the night of May 4th?"

RIE thought for a moment. "I was with a client."

Sato's expression didn't change. "Name?"

"Hold on."

RIE stood up and walked toward a shelf, pulling out a small file organizer. After flipping through a few pages, he stopped.

"Oh… yeah. Ruby Zeni."

Akane glanced at Sato.

Sato spoke carefully. "Ruby Zeni was found dead in her apartment."

RIE froze.

"And during the examination," Sato continued, "we discovered traces of your semen inside her body."

The room went silent.

RIE's face instantly lost color.

"What…?"

His voice shook. "Wait — I swear I didn't do anything!"

· · ·

Sato watched him closely. "Then explain."

RIE took a deep breath, trying to calm himself.

"I work in the adult film industry. Ruby hired me for a private video shoot. That's all. After that, we never contacted each other again."

He looked genuinely shaken. "Is… is she really dead?"

Akane answered quietly. "She was murdered in her apartment."

RIE sat there speechless.

??Either he was innocent… or an extremely good liar.

As RIE nervously rubbed his hands together, something suddenly caught Sato's attention.

A tattoo.

A thin black symbol running across the side of his wrist.

Sato's eyes narrowed slightly.

But he said nothing.

Not yet.

Instead, he stood up calmly. "That'll be all for now."

Akane blinked. "We're leaving?"

Sato ignored the question and looked toward RIE.

"Thank you for cooperating with the investigation."

RIE gave a weak nod. "No… problem."

· · ·

Later that night, while walking back toward the agency, Akane finally spoke.

"Sato… why did we leave so suddenly?"

Sato kept his hands in his pockets as he walked beneath the dim streetlights.

"This case is much bigger than we imagined."

Akane looked at him quietly.

Sato continued. "Ruby died on May 5th. According to the timeline, RIE was the last known person to see her alive."

He paused for a moment. "But something feels wrong."

"You think someone else entered the apartment later that night?" Akane asked.

Sato looked up at the dark sky.

"…Either that."

His expression hardened slightly.

"Or RIE is hiding something."
