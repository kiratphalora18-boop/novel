The Rain Pact
Chapter Four

When Umbrellas Stay Closed

🌧️

It rained again.
Not the gentle kind this time — this was heavy, angry rain, pounding against rooftops and sidewalks like the sky itself was grieving.
Aoi stood under the tree, soaking.
She didn't bring an umbrella.
She hadn't planned to be there at all, but her feet carried her after school without thought.
Habit, maybe. Or something more.
Itsuki hadn't come.
For three days now, even when it rained, he hadn't shown up.
She told herself it didn't matter. That people leave all the time. That she didn't need him.
But standing in the downpour now, her soaked uniform clinging to her skin, the truth ached quietly in her chest:
She missed him.

When she arrived home, her grandmother scolded her gently, wrapped her in a blanket, and handed her warm tea.
The television murmured in the background. Aoi didn't listen.
Instead, her eyes fell to her desk.
The paper crane was still there—untouched. She picked it up and turned it over in her fingers.
She remembered her mother once taught her to fold one.
Before the accident.
Before the silence.
Before Aoi stopped letting people get close.

The next day, Aoi didn't go to the tree. She took the long route home, headphones in, hood up.
But halfway across a bridge over the river, she saw him.
Itsuki.
He stood alone, holding the familiar blue umbrella — but it was closed. Rain drenched him.
He looked lost.
Their eyes met.
He smiled.
Aoi didn't know whether to be angry or relieved. She walked over, saying nothing.
He didn't speak either. He just held out the paper crane she had given him.
It was wrinkled. Water-stained. But intact.
"I… was visiting my mom," he said at last. "She's in the hospital. I didn't mean to vanish."
Aoi looked at him, the rain washing down both their faces, blurring everything.
And without a word, she opened the umbrella.
Together, they stood beneath it. No pact spoken. No promise made.
But sometimes, that was enough.

· · ·

The rain eased over the next few days. It didn't stop, just softened—like it, too, had run out of energy.
Aoi and Itsuki didn't walk together every day now. But when they did, it wasn't awkward.
No explanations. No apologies. Just a shared umbrella and the soft rhythm of shoes splashing in puddles.
One afternoon, during lunch, Aoi found a note in her locker.
It was folded into a triangle. On it, in the corner, was a tiny doodle of a crane.
"Meet me by the old music room after school. I want to show you something."
— Itsuki

She found him sitting at the piano.
The room hadn't been used in years, dust covering most of the keys, sunlight filtering through broken blinds.
"Do you play?" she asked quietly.
Itsuki shook his head. "Nope. But I used to sit here when I didn't want anyone to find me."
"Why bring me here, then?"
He didn't look at her. Just pressed a single key—soft, out of tune.
"Because I'm tired of being alone," he said. "And when I'm around you, it's… quiet. But not lonely."
Aoi's heart tugged.
She didn't know what to say. So she sat beside him, their shoulders barely touching.
They stared at the broken keys together.

"I don't like the rain," she whispered. "Not really."
Itsuki turned to her.
"My parents… We were in a car. It was raining. That's when everything changed."
She didn't cry. She didn't need to.
Itsuki reached out and placed the wrinkled paper crane on the piano's top.
"Then maybe we make new memories with the rain."
Aoi closed her eyes.
Outside, the drizzle started again.
Inside, two broken people sat in silence.
And the silence no longer felt empty.

— End of Chapter Four —
