Him and Her
Chapter Three

A Silent Place

✿
Kazuki — POV

I reached school at my usual time.
Honestly, I am a little lazy in the morning.
When I entered the classroom, I looked at the seat next to mine.
Sayori wasn't there.
That was surprising.
Usually she arrives earlier than most students.
But… it's normal to be late sometimes.
After all, she's human too.
I sat down and waited for class to begin.
But even after a few minutes…
Her seat was still empty.

✿   ·   ✿
Sayori — POV

I am late.
Very late.
I run down the street toward school, but slow down after a while.
If I go now…
Everyone will look at me.
They will ask questions.
They will expect things from me again.
Expectations.
That is the thing I hate the most.
I stop walking.
Maybe…
I should skip the first lecture.
Just for today.
I need somewhere quiet.
Somewhere peaceful.
Then a place comes to my mind.
That pink sakura tree.
Kazuki's quiet place.
Yeah…
I'll go there.

·   ·   ·
Kazuki — POV

The first lecture ended.
Students began leaving their seats, talking loudly like usual.
But Sayori still hadn't shown up.
I looked at her empty chair again.
Where is she?
Then the teacher announced something.
The second lecture is cancelled.
A free period.
I stood up and looked toward the door.
Maybe…
I should try to look for her.
For some reason, I had a feeling I knew where she might be.

Before going to the sakura tree, I decided to ask a few of Sayori's friends.
"Do you know where Sayori is?"
Some of them said they didn't know.
Others didn't even seem to care that she wasn't around.
That surprised me.
Everyone in class always gathers around her.
But the moment she isn't there…
No one really looks for her.
I wonder if Sayori even realizes that.
People often like attention and popularity, not the person behind it.
Maybe that's one more reason I don't trust friendships easily.

After that, I walked toward the place where I usually spend lunch.
The quiet spot.
Near the vending machines.
Under the pink sakura tree.
And just like I thought…
She was there.
Sitting alone on the bench.
Looking distant.
Almost like she was zoned out.

✿   ·   ✿
Near the pink tree bench

Kazuki: "Sayori… found you."
She looks up, a little surprised.
Sayori: "Oh… eh? Oh, it's you, Kazuki."
She straightens slightly.
Sayori: "What brings you here? Shouldn't you be in class?"
Kazuki: "Isn't the same true for you?"
I sit down beside her.
Kazuki: "Also, the second lecture got cancelled."
Sayori: "Oh…"
For a moment, she stays quiet.
Then she speaks again.
Sayori: "I didn't expect you to show up here."
Kazuki: "Well… I was curious."
I glance at her.
Kazuki: "A girl who usually arrives first in class wasn't there this morning."
Sayori lets out a small laugh.
Sayori: "Yeah… I got late."
She looks down at her hands.
Sayori: "So I skipped it."
Kazuki: "You stayed here the whole time?"
Sayori: "Yes."
She sighs softly.
Sayori: "I was afraid to show up."

She continues quietly.
Sayori: "What would the teachers think if I arrived late?"
Sayori: "What would my friends think?"
Sayori: "And…"
I interrupt gently.
Kazuki: "Don't worry, Sayori."
She looks at me.
Kazuki: "You're human too."
A small breeze moves the sakura petals around us.
Kazuki: "You're allowed to be late."
Kazuki: "You don't have to be perfect all the time."
She watches me silently.
Kazuki: "You're not here just to please people."
Sayori looks down again.
Sayori: "Am I…?"

I think about what happened earlier.
Kazuki: "Before coming here, I asked some of your friends where you were."
She looks up quickly.
Sayori: "What did they say?"
Kazuki: "Some didn't know."
I pause.
Kazuki: "And some didn't really care."
Sayori stays silent.
A few petals fall between us.
Kazuki: "People often care about what they find interesting."
Kazuki: "Popularity. Attention."
Kazuki: "Not always the person."
Sayori smiles sadly.
Sayori: "So… none of them said anything?"
Kazuki: "No, Sayori."

She looks at the tree above us.
Sayori: "Sometimes… I wish I had something like the life you live."
I shake my head slightly.
Kazuki: "Mine?"
Kazuki: "I bet you wouldn't be that happy if you had it."
She turns toward me again.
Then she smiles faintly.
Sayori: "What you have… you can share it with me, right?"
I look at her, confused.
Sayori speaks softly.
Sayori: "After all…"
She pauses.
Sayori: "I'm human too."
Sayori: "Aren't I… Kazuki?"
The sakura petals continue falling quietly around us.

— End of Chapter Three —
